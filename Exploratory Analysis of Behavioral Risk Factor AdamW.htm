Exploratory Analysis of Behavioral Risk Factor System

Introduction

This project was completed as part of a data science portfolio. In this project, the goal is to demonstrate exploratory data analysis with the BRFSS-2013 data set. First, several research questions are posed. Then relationship between identified variables is explored to answer those questions.

Packages and Data Set

Loading the packages

library(ggplot2)

library(dplyr)

Loading the data set

load("brfss2013.RData")

 

The BRFSS-2013 data set can be found at https://www.cdc.gov/brfss/. This data was obtained by sampling from the non-institutionalized adult population (18 years and older) who live within the US. The data was obtained via surveys over the phone using landline and cellular phone numbers.

Disproportionate stratified sampling was utilized to obtain the landline sample. The cell phone sample was produced through randomly selected respondents, with an equal probability of selection.

As random sampling was used for both data collection methods, the data for the sample is generalizable to the population. On the other hand, as this is an observational study, it wont be possible to make causal inferences from the data.

List of Research Questions

First question:

Are people who dont smoke but drink heavily healthier than people who regularly smoke and do not drink heavily?

While researching this, the goal is to determine the impact of consuming alcohol vs smoking tobacco on a persons health to see which has a worse impact.

Second question:

Do people who sleep less hours than the average, suffer from more days of poor mental health?

Research hints that a lack of sleep negatively impacts the overall health of a person. In this research, one of the goals is to determine if a lack of sleep has a negative impact on mental health.

Third question:

Are people with more levels of education more likely to eat fruits and vegetables?

Some people might assume that educated individuals have healthier lifestyles, such as exercising or eating nutritious food. Another goal of this research is to determine the impact of completing more levels of education on the choice to consume fruits and vegetables daily.

Second question:

Do people who sleep less hours than the average, suffer from more days of poor mental health?

To analyze this, we have to look at the relationship between the variables:

  • sleptim1: On average, the hours of sleep a person gets in a 24-hour period.
  • menthlth: Out of 30, number of days the mental health of a person wasnt good.

Third question:

Are people who have completed higher levels of education more likely to consume fruits and vegetables once or more in a day?

For this well be using the following variables:

  • _educag: Computed level of education completed.
  • _frtlt1: Consume fruit 1 or times per day.
  • _veglt1: Consume vegetables 1 or times per day.
